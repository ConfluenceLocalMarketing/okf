# Frequently Asked Questions - Basil Mobility

## Vehicles & Conversions

**How often should a wheelchair accessible van be serviced?**

Most mobility vehicles benefit from routine maintenance every 6 to 12 months. Regular inspections help ensure the vehicle and accessibility equipment continue to operate safely.

**Do wheelchair accessible vans require special maintenance?**

Yes. In addition to standard automotive maintenance, accessible vans require inspections of lifts, ramps, power doors, and other accessibility systems.

**What BraunAbility conversions are available?**

Basil Mobility offers the BraunAbility E2 (Chrysler Pacifica/Voyager, power fold-out ramp), XT (Toyota Sienna, power fold-out ramp), and XI (Chrysler Pacifica/Voyager, in-floor ramp). A new Chevrolet Traverse conversion is coming in 2026.

**What is the difference between side-entry and rear-entry vans?**

Side-entry vans have the ramp at the sliding door, ideal for standard wheelchairs. Rear-entry vans load through the back, better for tilter/transporter chairs. Basil Mobility carries both types.

**Can I buy a lowered-floor vehicle?**

Yes - lowered-floor vehicles provide better interior clearance for wheelchair access and are available on both Chrysler and Toyota platforms.

**Do you carry hybrid accessible vans?**

Yes - the BraunAbility XT conversion on the Toyota Sienna is a hybrid, offering fuel efficiency with full accessibility.

**Are wheelchair van rentals available?**

Yes - Basil Mobility offers short and long-term accessible van rentals.

## Adaptive Equipment

**Can you install hand controls?**

Yes - Basil Mobility offers custom fitting and installation of adaptive hand controls for driving.

**What other adaptive equipment is available?**

Steering aids, transfer seats (BraunAbility Turny), wheelchair lifts, power ramp systems, securement tie-downs, and custom interior modifications.

## Service & Repairs

**Can you repair wheelchair lifts that are not working properly?**

Yes. Technicians can diagnose lift issues including electrical faults, mechanical problems, or hydraulic system failures.

**What causes a wheelchair ramp to stop working?**

Ramp problems can be caused by electrical issues, motor failures, worn components, or alignment problems that require professional inspection.

**Can you service power door systems?**

Yes - the service center handles power sliding door and rear door diagnostics and repair.

**What routine maintenance services do mobility vans need?**

Routine services include oil changes, filter replacements (engine/cabin), fluid checks, tire inspections, brake service, and general vehicle diagnostics.

**Can non-mobility vehicles be serviced?**

Yes - the service center also handles standard auto repair for non-mobility vehicles.

**Do you perform safety inspections for paratransit vehicles?**

Yes - comprehensive commercial vehicle safety inspections for paratransit shuttle vans are available.

## Financing

**What financing options are available?**

Basil Mobility offers APR as low as 2.99% for up to 72 months through BraunAbility Financial Services. Up to $3,000 dealer cash may be available. Lease options are also offered.

**Can I get financing with low income or bad credit?**

Yes - Basil Mobility offers low-income auto loan approvals and works with buyers with various credit situations.

**Can I value my trade-in online?**

Yes - an online trade-in appraisal tool is available on the Basil Mobility website.

**Are there grants available for mobility vehicles?**

Yes - BraunAbility Financial Services and Basil Mobility can assist with grant applications, state and local funding programs, and disability advocacy organization resources.

## Dealership

**What area does Basil Mobility serve?**

Basil Mobility serves Depew, Buffalo, Cheektowaga, Williamsville, Amherst, and the greater Western New York area.

**Does Basil Mobility offer rentals?**

Yes - short and long-term accessible van rentals are available.

**Is there a courtesy shuttle during service?**

Yes - a two-way courtesy shuttle is available during service appointments.
